# Wave 3 Vertical Configurations — 2026-05-16

These four verticals are picked for LOW-PE-ATTENTION + HIGH-RECURRING-REVENUE characteristics — like land surveying and septic OSSF that emerged as the highest-yield verticals in prior runs.

## WAVE 3

### W3-A. Welding / metal fabrication shops (NAICS 332710 / 332323)

**Why this vertical:** Highly aging trade (median owner age in US metal-fab is 58). Niche industrial / commercial customers (often single-source supplier relationships = stable recurring). LOW-PE-ATTENTION = low entry multiples (3-4x SDE typical). Strong SBA 7(a) financeability. Many TX shops serve oil & gas / petrochem / construction supply chains.

**Spine source:**
- **No state-specific license** for welding/metal fab in TX beyond business registration.
- **Primary:** Texas Comptroller franchise tax entity search by NAICS code 332710 / 332999 / 332323. Search at `mycpa.cpa.state.tx.us/staxpayersearch`.
- **Secondary:** Google "[city] metal fabrication" + "[city] welding shop" + "[city] custom metalwork."
- **Tertiary:** AWS (American Welding Society) certified welder directory. NAM (National Association of Manufacturers) TX chapter directory.
- **Quaternary:** Texas Manufacturers Association directory.

**NAICS + filters:**
- NAICS: 332710 (Machine Shops), 332323 (Ornamental and Architectural Metal Work Manufacturing), 332999 (Other Misc Fabricated Metal Product Mfg), 332313 (Plate Work Mfg).
- **Exclude large fabricators** (>$50M revenue, often public or large-PE-owned).
- **Exclude oil-services-only fabricators** (cyclical, hard to value).
- **Include:** Custom metal fab job shops, structural steel for construction, architectural metalwork (handrails, gates, ornamental), trailer mfg, industrial misc. 5-50 employees.

**Successor candidate definition:**
- Family member on staff (often son) → STRONG successor (very common in metal trades).
- Long-tenured shop foreman / lead welder → MEDIUM successor.
- Solo owner-welder → NO SUCCESSOR (target).

**Recurring-revenue language (Layer 2):**
- "production runs," "scheduled production," "long-term supplier agreement."
- "annual maintenance fab," "shutdown fab work" (refinery / petrochem recurring).
- "custom architectural metalwork," "structural steel," "ornamental gates," "custom trailers."
- "DOT trailer repair," "commercial trailer fab."
- Healthy mix: 40-70% repeat customers, 30-60% project / one-off.

**Coasting tells (Layer 3):**
- Same building since 1970s/80s/90s; owner owns the building.
- Aging equipment visible in shop photos (still using older Miller/Lincoln equipment from 1990s-2000s, no newer Plasma cutters, no CNC waterjet, no fiber laser, no Hypertherm modern systems).
- Website built in 2008-2015 era (or no website).
- No CAD / Solidworks / Inventor / Fusion 360 visible (modern shops mention CAD-driven manufacturing).
- No "We're hiring welders" / no apprenticeship program (industry-wide labor shortage means healthy shops are always recruiting).
- Owner is also a welder / fabricator (carrying field load).
- No social media / no project portfolio posts.
- Reviews thin or stale.

**Active acquirer platforms (Layer 4):**
- **PE-backed national consolidators (less active than other trades but growing):**
  - **Atlas Air Worldwide / Atlas Industries** (general industrial roll-ups)
  - **Frontline Road Safety** (industrial signage / fab)
- **Strategic acquirers:**
  - Larger metal fab companies acquiring competitors regionally
  - **Trinity Industries** (Dallas-based, large fabricator — may acquire small shops)
  - **Tutle & Tutle Industries**
  - **Texas Iron Works**
- **SBA 7(a) financeability:** YES, multiples 3-4x SDE for $200K-$1M SDE.
- **ETA / search-fund appetite:** Niche but rising (low-multiple, asset-heavy can be opportunity for asset-backed lending).
- Multiples: 3-4x SDE typical for low-PE-attention shops; 4-6x EBITDA for higher-recurring.

**SBA-size estimation:**
- Solo welder + 1-2 helpers ≈ $300K-$700K rev.
- 5-10 employee shop ≈ $700K-$2.5M.
- 10-30 employee shop ≈ $2.5M-$8M.
- EBITDA: 10-20%.

**Sub-market nudges (Layer 4):**
- **Industrial / petrochem corridors (Houston Ship Channel, Beaumont/Port Arthur, Corpus Christi):** +5 (recurring shutdown / repair fab work).
- **DFW (general industrial):** +3.
- **Austin/SA (less heavy industry):** -3.
- **East TX:** +0 (oil & gas servicing).
- **Hill Country / rural:** -3.

### W3-B. Glass services — flat + shower + auto-replacement (NAICS 238150 / 811122)

**Why this vertical:** Mixed segment. Auto-glass arm under Safelite/Speedy Glass pressure (consolidated). FLAT GLASS + SHOWER GLASS + STOREFRONT GLASS = fragmented, owner-operated, recurring commercial install + replacement, plus residential shower-glass install (high margin). Aging owners. Low PE attention on the non-auto segment = good buy entry.

**Spine source:**
- **No state license required** for glass install in TX beyond contractor general liability.
- **Primary:** Google "[city] glass company" + "[city] shower door installation" + "[city] commercial glazier" + "[city] storefront glass."
- **Secondary:** GANA (Glass Association of North America) member directory `glassmagazine.com/membership`.
- **Tertiary:** AAMA (American Architectural Mfg Assn) member directory.

**NAICS + filters:**
- NAICS: 238150 (Glass and Glazing Contractors), 811122 (Automotive Glass Replacement Shops).
- **Exclude auto-glass-only chains:**
  - **Safelite AutoGlass** (Belron / Safelite group — dominant)
  - **Speedy Glass** (Belron)
  - **Glass Doctor** (Neighborly franchise)
- **Include:** Flat glass + storefront + commercial glazing, shower-glass install specialists, mixed glass (flat + auto), independent local glass.

**Successor candidate definition:**
- Family member on staff → STRONG.
- Long-tenured shop manager → MEDIUM.
- Solo owner-installer → NO (target).

**Recurring-revenue language (Layer 2):**
- "commercial glazing contract," "office tenant fit-out glass."
- "shower door installation," "frameless shower glass."
- "storefront repair / replacement" (recurring needs-based).
- "insurance glass replacement" (auto).
- Healthy mix: 40-60% commercial install / replace (recurring), 20-40% residential shower / window, 10-30% repair work.

**Coasting tells (Layer 3):**
- Same brand since 1980s-90s.
- Aging fleet.
- No online quote / no portal.
- Owner in field.
- No newer architectural-glass capability (no curved glass, no SGP laminates).

**Active acquirer platforms (Layer 4):**
- **PE-backed for auto-only:** Belron / Safelite (dominant)
- **For flat / commercial: LOW PE ACTIVITY = ENTRY OPPORTUNITY.**
- **Strategic acquirers:**
  - Larger regional glass cos
- **SBA 7(a):** YES.
- **ETA appetite:** LOW (under-the-radar = good for entry, less acquirer competition for the buy).
- Multiples: 3-4x SDE; 4-5x EBITDA.

**SBA-size estimation:**
- Solo 1-2 trucks $300K-$700K rev.
- 3-8 employees $700K-$2.5M.
- 10+ employees $2.5M-$8M.
- EBITDA: 10-20%.

**Sub-market nudges (Layer 4):**
- **DFW + Austin + Houston commercial-heavy:** +3.
- **Premium resi (Sugar Land, Plano, Highland Park):** +3 (shower-glass install).
- **Rural / coastal:** 0.

### W3-C. Janitorial / commercial cleaning (NAICS 561720)

**Why this vertical:** Largest small-business sector by count. Recurring contract revenue (commercial cleaning is per-month, multi-year contracts). Many TX shops Hispanic-owned, family-run, aging owners. Hot consolidator pressure (Coverall, JaniKing, ServiceMaster, ABM Industries, GCA Services, Pritchard Industries). SBA 7(a) financeable.

**Spine source:**
- **No state license required** beyond business registration.
- **Primary:** ISSA (Worldwide Cleaning Industry Association) member directory.
- **Secondary:** Google "[city] commercial cleaning" + "[city] janitorial services" + "[city] office cleaning."
- **Tertiary:** BSCAI (Building Service Contractors Association International) directory.

**NAICS + filters:**
- NAICS: 561720 (Janitorial Services).
- **Exclude franchise locations:**
  - **JaniKing**
  - **Coverall**
  - **Office Pride**
  - **Stratus Building Solutions**
  - **JAN-PRO**
  - **Vanguard Cleaning Systems**
  - **Anago Cleaning Systems**
- **Exclude national-account-only B2B large vendors:**
  - **ABM Industries** (public)
  - **ServiceMaster Clean** (chain)
  - **GCA Services** (acquired by ABM)
  - **Pritchard Industries**
- **Include:** Independent local commercial cleaning, 10-200 employees, family-owned. Industry-standard for small B2B office cleaning, medical office cleaning, retail cleaning.

**Successor candidate definition:**
- Family member as operations director → STRONG.
- Long-tenured ops manager → MEDIUM.
- Solo owner → NO (target).

**Recurring-revenue language (Layer 2):**
- "monthly office cleaning contract," "5x per week janitorial."
- "medical office cleaning," "HIPAA-compliant cleaning."
- "school janitorial contract," "church cleaning contract."
- "post-construction cleanup" (one-time, less valuable but high-margin).
- "carpet cleaning," "floor stripping & waxing" (recurring annual/quarterly).
- Healthy mix: 70-90% recurring monthly contracts.

**Coasting tells (Layer 3):**
- Same brand since 1990s.
- No website / website pre-2015.
- No customer portal / no work-order app.
- No employee management tech (no Janitorial Manager, Clean Easier visible).
- Phone-only intake.
- Owner is also doing cleaning.
- No social media.
- No "we're hiring" listings (industry-standard high turnover; absence = under-utilized capacity).

**Active acquirer platforms (Layer 4):**
- **PE-backed national consolidators:**
  - **ABM Industries** (NYSE: ABM)
  - **Pritchard Industries** (PE)
  - **Aramark** (public; some janitorial)
  - **GCA Services** (now ABM)
  - **C&W Services**
  - **Diversified Maintenance** (PE)
- **TX-active regional:**
  - **Texas Pure Cleaning** (regional)
- **SBA 7(a):** YES.
- **ETA appetite:** RISING (recurring B2B revenue model).
- Multiples: 3-5x SDE; 4-6x EBITDA at $1M+ EBITDA.

**SBA-size estimation:**
- Solo 5-10 employees $300K-$700K rev.
- 15-30 employees $700K-$2M.
- 30-100 employees $2M-$6M.
- 100+ employees $6M+.
- EBITDA: 8-18% (labor-heavy).

**Sub-market nudges (Layer 4):**
- **DFW + Austin commercial-heavy:** +5
- **Houston metro:** +3
- **SA:** +0
- **Suburban office-park-heavy (Plano, Sugar Land, Round Rock):** +3
- **Rural:** -5

### W3-D. Industrial machine shops — CNC (NAICS 332710)

**Why this vertical:** Same family as W3-A welding but specifically CNC-machining-focused shops (different customer mix, different equipment). Highly aging trade, niche industrial customer base, low PE attention. Owner-operator with $500K-$3M CNC equipment investment = high-asset-backed-collateral business.

**Spine source:**
- **No state license required.**
- **Primary:** TX Comptroller franchise tax entity search by NAICS 332710.
- **Secondary:** Google "[city] CNC machine shop" + "[city] precision machining" + "[city] CNC milling turning."
- **Tertiary:** PMPA (Precision Machined Products Association) member directory. NTMA (National Tooling and Machining Association) directory.

**NAICS + filters:**
- NAICS: 332710 (Machine Shops).
- **Sub-vertical:** Specifically PRECISION CNC (turning, milling, EDM, grinding). Differentiate from W3-A welding shops.
- **Exclude large fabricators** (>$30M revenue).
- **Include:** Job shops with 5-50 employees, CNC + manual, supplier to oil & gas / aerospace / medical / industrial OEM.

**Successor candidate definition:**
- Family member as shop manager → STRONG.
- Long-tenured lead machinist → MEDIUM.
- Solo owner-machinist → NO (target).

**Recurring-revenue language (Layer 2):**
- "ISO 9001 certified," "AS9100 certified" (aerospace), "ITAR registered" (defense).
- "long-term supply agreement," "JIT (just-in-time) delivery."
- "prototype to production runs," "production CNC work."
- "low-volume / high-mix manufacturing," "high-volume production CNC."
- Healthy mix: 60-80% repeat customer recurring.

**Coasting tells (Layer 3):**
- Same shop since 1970s/80s/90s; aging equipment (Mazak / Haas / Mori-Seiki 1990s-2000s vs. modern Hardinge, Kitamura, Okuma 2018+).
- No CAD/CAM (no Mastercam, Fusion 360, SolidCAM, GibbsCAM mentioned).
- Website built 2008-2015 (or no website).
- No ISO 9001 if mostly serving aerospace/medical (= leaving margin on table).
- Owner is also the lead machinist.
- No new equipment investment in 5+ yrs.
- No "we're hiring" / no apprenticeship.

**Active acquirer platforms (Layer 4):**
- **PE-backed national consolidators (less active in small CNC):**
  - **Premium Manufacturing Group** (PE)
  - **TruePoint Solutions**
  - **Wesco Industries**
- **Strategic acquirers:**
  - **Trinity Industries** (Dallas)
  - Larger CNC shops acquiring competitors regionally
- **SBA 7(a):** YES (asset-backed especially).
- **ETA appetite:** NICHE but rising for ETA buyers with engineering backgrounds.
- Multiples: 3-4x SDE; 4-6x EBITDA at $1M+.

**SBA-size estimation:**
- 5-10 employee shop ≈ $700K-$2M rev.
- 10-30 employees ≈ $2M-$8M.
- EBITDA: 10-25%.

**Sub-market nudges (Layer 4):**
- **Houston metro (oil & gas + petrochem):** +5
- **DFW (general industrial + aerospace):** +5
- **Austin (semicon / aerospace adjacent):** +5
- **SA (aerospace/defense, Kelly AFB area):** +3
- **East TX:** +0
- **Rural:** -3
